Biome Overview

The tagia biome is a biome with cold winters and mild summers, tipically found in northern Russia and Canada. The wildlife found in tagias is usually lareg mammals and other species adapted to the cold.

Abotic Factors

The abiotic factors of the tagia biome are very cold winters, low precipitation, permafrost, and acidic soil.

Biotic Factors

The biotic factors of the tagia are large pine trees, spruce trees, and sometimes birches. The wildlife found in the tagias is usually, moose, brown bears, deer, lnyxes.
