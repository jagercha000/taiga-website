Biome Overview

The taiga biome is the largest terrestrial biome on Earth, and is a biome with cold winters and mild summers, tipically found in northern Russia and Canada. The wildlife found in taigas is usually large mammals and other species adapted to the cold.

Abotic Factors

The abiotic factors of the taiga biome are very cold winters, down to -40 degrees Fahrenheit in northern Siberia (sometimes warmer in Canada), low precipitation, often 8 to 31 inches, permafrost, and acidic soil, making it hard for certain plants to grow.

Biotic Factors

The biotic factors of the taiga are large pine trees, spruce trees, and sometimes birches. The wildlife found in the taigas is usually, moose, brown bears, deer, lnyxes.
