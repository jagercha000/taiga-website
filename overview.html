Biome Overview

The taiga biome is the largest terrestrial biome on Earth, and is a biome with cold winters and mild summers, typically found in northern Russia and Canada. The wildlife found in taigas is usually large mammals and other species adapted to the cold.

Abotic Factors

The abiotic factors of the taiga biome are very cold winters, down to -40 degrees Fahrenheit in northern Siberia (sometimes warmer in Canada), low precipitation, often 8 to 31 inches, permafrost, and acidic soil, making it hard for certain plants to grow.

Biotic Factors

The biotic factors of the taiga are birch tree (producer), moose (prey), brown bears (preditor), deer (prey), lnyxes (preditor), ligonberries (producer), and mushrooms (decomposer).

Predator and Prey

A predator and prey relationship in the taiga is the deer and the wolf. The wolf needs the deer to survive and get it's food and the deer must avoid the wolf.
