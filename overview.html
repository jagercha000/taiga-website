Biome Overview

The taiga biome is the largest terrestrial biome on Earth, and is a biome with cold winters and mild summers, typically found in northern Russia and Canada. The wildlife found in taigas is usually large mammals and other species adapted to the cold.

Climate

The climate of the taiga biome includes very cold winters, with temperatures dropping to -40 degrees Fahrenheit in northern Siberia, low precipitation, permafrost, and acidic soil.

Animals

Some animals that live in taigas are birch trees, moose, brown bears, deer, lynxes, lingonberries, and mushrooms.

Predators and Prey

Predators in taigas include bears, lynxes, and wolves.
